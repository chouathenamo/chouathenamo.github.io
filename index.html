Hi! I'm Athena, an undergraduate researcher at UCLA studying Mathematics of Computation. My research sits at the intersection of computer vision and medical imaging — I'm interested in building robust, data-efficient models that can assist clinicians in surgical planning and image-guided interventions.

I currently work in the Medical & Imaging Informatics (MII) lab under Dr. William Hsu, where I focus on intra-patient registration of lung CT with optimal transport methods. Previously, I was a VISE Summer Fellow at Vanderbilt University in the VINE lab under Dr. Daniel Moyer, where I developed a pelvic pose estimation pipeline for orthopedic surgical planning.

Outside of research, I lead the programming team for UCLA's University Rover Challenge team, co-lead a data science project at the UCLA Data Science Union, and sail competitively.
